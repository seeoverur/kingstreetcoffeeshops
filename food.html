Food Places That Sell Coffee on King Street

Not every great cup of coffee comes from a dedicated café. Some of the best coffee on King Street is poured at restaurants, brunch spots, and casual eateries that take their drinks just as seriously as their food.

Where Good Food Meets Great Coffee

Charleston's King Street dining scene has quietly become one of the best places in the Southeast to eat and drink well — and that includes the coffee. Across the strip and surrounding blocks, you'll find breakfast joints, brunch spots, sandwich counters, and casual lunch spots where the espresso machine gets as much attention as the kitchen.

This page is dedicated to those hybrid spots: places where you came for the food and left thinking about the latte. Whether you want a full eggs-and-toast breakfast with a proper flat white, or a quick sandwich and an iced coffee before an afternoon of shopping, King Street has exactly what you're looking for.

Brunch & Coffee

The Morning Table

Open Now Upper King

A proper sit-down brunch spot where the coffee program gets the same care as the kitchen. The espresso is sourced locally, and the drip is always fresh. Order something off the egg menu and a cortado — you won't regret it.

Espresso · Drip · Cold Brew
Sandwiches & Café

Counter & Press

Open Now Lower King

Pressed sandwiches, seasonal soups, and a surprisingly excellent latte bar tucked into the corner. The lunch crowd fills it up quickly — get here early or expect a short wait that's absolutely worth it.

Lattes · Cappuccino · Iced Coffee
Casual Eats

Plate & Perch

Open Now King & Calhoun

A fast-casual spot that refuses to cut corners on the drink side. The house drip rotates weekly, and the oat milk latte has earned a devoted following among the lunch regulars who come back every weekday.

Drip · Lattes · Oat Milk Options
Bakery & Espresso

Flour & Fold

Open Now Cannonborough

Artisan pastries and fresh-baked breads paired with a tight espresso menu. The croissant and flat white combination is the kind of thing that makes a slow Saturday morning feel like a special occasion.

Flat White · Espresso · Macchiato
Breakfast All Day

Early Bird Kitchen

Open Now King Street

All-day breakfast served with coffee that's actually worth ordering. The avocado toast and a vanilla latte is the house pairing everyone seems to end up with — and there's a good reason for that.

Lattes · Drip · Seasonal Specials
Lunch & Cold Brew

The Noon Break

Open Now Upper King

Built for the midday crowd: fast, fresh lunch options alongside cold brew on tap and a rotating menu of iced lattes. The grain bowls are popular, and the coffee keeps the afternoon going.

Cold Brew · Iced Latte · Nitro

Why Coffee & Food Go Together

A Complete Experience

The best meals deserve the best drinks to go with them. A well-made espresso or a creamy latte rounds out a meal in a way that nothing else quite does.

One Stop, Done

When your food spot also does great coffee, you don't need to plan two separate stops. Eat well, drink well, get on with your day — King Street makes that easy.

Built for Lingering

Food-and-coffee spots tend to encourage you to stay a little longer. They're designed for conversation, for catching up, for turning a meal into an actual break in your day.

Quality Across the Board

A place that takes its food seriously usually takes its coffee seriously too. The standards that make a great kitchen tend to carry through to everything on the menu.

Types of Food & Coffee Spots on King Street

King Street isn't short on options when it comes to eating and drinking well in the same place. Here's a breakdown of the main formats you'll find in the neighborhood.

Breakfast & Brunch Cafés

Morning-focused spots that serve full menus alongside a proper espresso bar. These tend to be the best of both worlds — kitchen quality food with barista-level coffee.

Counter Service & Delis

Quick-service spots built for the lunch rush. Sandwiches, wraps, and grain bowls paired with iced lattes or drip coffee. Fast without being careless.

Bakeries with Espresso Bars

Fresh-baked goods and a focused coffee menu. Usually smaller and more neighborhood-oriented — the kind of place locals guard jealously and tell visiting friends about quietly.

All-Day Casual Eateries

Open from morning through late afternoon, these spots serve a flexible menu — light bites, heavier plates, coffee drinks, and often energy drinks — all under one roof.
